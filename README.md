## Step 3: Mean & Variance
- Mean and variance were computed at each pixel across the first **T** frames:  
  - snowFall → 45 frames  
  - person3 → 60 frames  
  - person1 → 70 frames  

- **Results:**  
  - Mean image saved as `mean.png`.  
  - Variance (visualized with normalization) saved as `variance.png`.

---

## Step 4: Change Detection (Distance Thresholding)
- Mahalanobis distance was used:  
  \[
  D(x,y) = \frac{(I(x,y) - \mu(x,y))^2}{\sigma^2(x,y)}
  \]  

- A binary mask is generated by thresholding \( D(x,y) > T \).  
- Experiments were done with thresholds **2.0**, **5.0**, and **8.0**.  
- Observation:  
  - Low threshold (2.0): detects more foreground but introduces noise.  
  - Medium threshold (5.0): balanced detection.  
  - High threshold (8.0): fewer detections, risk of missing small changes.  

---

## Step 5: Morphological Operations
- To remove noise, erosion and dilation were implemented manually.  
- Tested with kernel sizes **5x5**, **7x7**, **11x11**.  
- Observation:  
  - Small kernel (5x5): retains small blobs, less smooth.  
  - Larger kernel (7x7, 11x11): smoother masks, but can over-smooth.  
- Example masks saved in output folder.

---

## Step 6: Connected Components
- A flood-fill algorithm was used to label blobs.  
- Each component’s area, centroid, and bounding box were computed.  
- Useful for identifying separate moving objects.

---

## Step 7: Removing Person (Alpha Blending)
- Gradual removal of the detected person was performed with alpha blending:  
  \[
  F_{\text{new}} = k \cdot (1 - \alpha \cdot M) + t \cdot (\alpha \cdot M)
  \]  
  where  
  - \(k\): current frame (with person),  
  - \(t\): background (mean image),  
  - \(M\): binary mask,  
  - \(\alpha \in [0,1]\): blending factor.  

- Result: the person fades out smoothly over the sequence.  
- Output frames saved in `removed_frames/`.  
- Video saved as `removed_person.mp4`.

---

## Step 8: Saving Masks and Frames
- Custom PNG writer was implemented (no OpenCV, no PIL writing).  
- Both **masks** and **processed frames** are saved.  
- Final video generated using `imageio`.

---

## Observations
- Change detection works well when background is static.  
- Morphological operations significantly improve mask quality.  
- Alpha blending provides visually smooth removal.  
- Threshold and kernel size need tuning per dataset.
